Summary: Latitude vs. Wind Speed

The analysis of the latitude versus wind speed occured on May 1, 2018. On this day, the data show that the majority of the randomly generated cities had high wind speeds < 20-60 mph. The month of May started off with a bang as severe thunderstorms containing large hail and tornadoes struck the central Plains on Tuesday and Tuesday night.

Contributing factors to wind speed include, thunderstorms and tornadoes. Tornadoes are more common in United States than in any other country, receives more than 1,200 tornadoes annually which occurs March through June. As spring passes and summer begins, the mass of warm moist air moves northwest into the Great Plains and Midwestern states. During the months of May and June ("tornado season"), tornado activity is as its peak in the southern Great Plains. The air mass then moves northward into the Northern Great Plains and the Great Lakes area, causing a tornado activity peak in these areas during the summer months. During the late summer and early fall months, tornado activity in the United States tapers off. This is due to the relatively small difference between the temperature at the boundary of the hot air mass and the cool air mass at that time and an extension of the Bermuda High sitting over parts of the United States.
